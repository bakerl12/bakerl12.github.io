About me

I graduated with my PhD in Mechanical Engineering from the Harvard John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences in May 2025. I was a member of the Biodesign Lab advised by Professor Conor Walsh. I received my bachelor's degree in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Delaware in 2016.

My main research field is gait biomechanics, spanning different populations from competitive athletes to clinical groups, such as people post-stroke. I have over ten years of experience in human subjects research and motion capture analysis. During my PhD, I worked on lower limb locomotor sensing and estimation, with applications in running form feedback and assistive device efficacy. I am interested in using wearable sensing technologies and data science to understand human movement patterns and inform strategies for performance optimization.
